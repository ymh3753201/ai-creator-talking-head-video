# Viral Teardown

Use this when the user provides a viral video, screenshot, title, cover, transcript, or competitor account example.

## Teardown Checklist

1. First 3 seconds:
   - what promise, conflict, result, or curiosity is created;
   - whether the face, title, screenshot, or motion carries the hook.
2. Structure:
   - hook;
   - setup;
   - proof/example;
   - turning point;
   - conclusion or CTA.
3. Emotion curve:
   - curiosity;
   - surprise;
   - anxiety;
   - relief;
   - usefulness;
   - identity resonance.
4. Visual rhythm:
   - talking-head framing;
   - B-roll frequency;
   - screenshot/recording usage;
   - zooms, cuts, pauses, and title cards.
5. Subtitle style:
   - large title;
   - keyword highlights;
   - word-by-word captions;
   - bilingual captions;
   - safe area.
6. Cover/title logic:
   - concrete result;
   - conflict;
   - audience identity;
   - curiosity gap;
   - visual proof.

## Output Reusable Template

Provide a reusable template like:

```text
Hook: [contrarian claim]
Beat 1: [audience pain]
Beat 2: [example/proof]
Beat 3: [method]
Beat 4: [result/CTA]
Visual rhythm: [talking-head + screenshot + keyword card]
Subtitle rule: [large keywords and bottom captions]
```

## Anti-Copy Rule

Do not copy the competitor's wording, personal story, face identity, brand elements, or unique title. Extract structure, timing, and content mechanics only.

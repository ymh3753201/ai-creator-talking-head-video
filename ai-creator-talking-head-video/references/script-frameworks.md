# Script Frameworks

## Short Talking-Head Script

Use this structure for 15, 30, and 60-second videos:

1. Title: platform-native and specific.
2. Opening hook: result, conflict, question, contrarian claim, or mistake.
3. Context: why the audience should care now.
4. Main beats: 2 to 4 points, each with a visual or example.
5. Transition lines: short sentence that moves between beats.
6. Key captions: words that should appear large or highlighted.
7. CTA: comment, save, follow, watch full version, download material, or try the method.
8. Shot and B-roll notes.

## Platform Rewrite Rules

- Douyin: compress setup, speak in punchy lines, put the strongest claim first.
- Xiaohongshu: add personal experience, checklist, and practical nuance.
- Bilibili: keep logic and evidence, allow a slower first explanation after the hook.
- YouTube Shorts: use plain English, one point, clear payoff.
- YouTube horizontal: write chapters, examples, and retention reminders.

## Hook Patterns

- Result first: "我用一个方法，把..."
- Mistake first: "大多数人做这个视频，第一步就错了。"
- Contrast: "不是工具不行，是你选题方式不对。"
- Curiosity: "这个看起来普通的操作，真正厉害的是后面这一步。"
- Authority without bragging: "我拆了 20 条同类视频，发现爆款都在用同一个结构。"

## Longform Structure

Use for 2-minute, 5-minute, or longer videos:

- cold open;
- problem setup;
- chapter promise;
- chapter 1: core idea;
- chapter 2: example or demo;
- chapter 3: method or framework;
- summary;
- CTA and next video hook.

## Script Output Contract

When writing or rewriting a script, include:

- supplied-material and original-draft audit;
- preserved facts and intent, unsupported claims, and meaningful rewrite decisions;
- title;
- opening hook;
- complete production-ready spoken script;
- transition lines;
- key subtitle lines;
- B-roll or screen-recording suggestions only when effects are explicitly enabled; otherwise record effects as disabled and do not invent insert shots;
- avatar delivery notes;
- CTA.

Do not polish unsupported numbers, guarantees, policies, deadlines, or outcomes into authoritative copy. Remove them, qualify them, or mark them as needing evidence. When the requested duration and supplied draft do not fit, professionally optimize the copy and state the expected spoken duration; do not silently rush or extend the video.
